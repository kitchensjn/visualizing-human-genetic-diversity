Like Venn diagrams, Euler diagrams are a method for visualizing the overlap in groups. Euler diagrams have the added property that the areas of the overlaps are proportional to the amount shared between the respective groups.

The area percentage of each region is equal to the size of that area divided by the total area of the diagram.

Now, let's build a figure from scratch using an example dataset. Say we have a classroom of 150 students and we group them in two categories - long versus short hair and wearing a hat versus not. The above table shows the numbers of students in each category; for our diagram, we are going to focus just on the highlighted cells within the table.

There are 75 students who have long hair; 25 of those students are not wearing a hat and 50 are wearing a hat. At the same time, there are 75 students who are wearing a hat; 25 of those students have short hair and 50 have long hair. So the area of each circle is 75 square units and the area of the overlap should be 50 square units (or 50% of the total area of the figure). Drag these circles so that they overlap accordingly.
